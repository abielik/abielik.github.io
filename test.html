The basics

How it works

Each day you're matched against one opponent. You each make 5 prop picks from a shared pool of 10 lines. More points wins the matchup. Simple loop, deep strategy.

1
Daily
10 props are posted for the day
The commissioner drops the day's available lines — player props across NBA games. Each line has an over and an under, with real Vegas odds attached. Lines are live, meaning they can shift before you lock in.
2
Daily
You choose 5 props and assign confidence
Pick 5 of the 10 available props. For each one, choose over or under. Then assign a confidence score of 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5 — each number used exactly once across your 5 picks. Deadline is when the first game tips off.
3
Daily
Choose your "Double Down" and "Prop Blocker"
Assign your "Double Down" to one pick to potentially double your points, and use your "Prop Blocker" to block your opponent from selecting a specific prop (limiting them to 4 props if used correctly). If used strategically, these tools can significantly impact the outcome of your matchup.
4
Daily
Games play out, points are tallied
Correct picks earn points based on the odds of the line you locked in. Harder lines pay more. The person with more total points wins the matchup for the day. Highest daily scorer in the league wins a prize.
5
Nightly
Results posted, next day opens
Scores are announced, standings updated, and the next day's props drop. Over the full trial, the best record (total points as tiebreaker) determines the winner.
Points

How scoring works

Points come directly from the odds. A pick at +150 earns 1.50 pts if correct. A pick at -200 earns 0.50 pts if correct. Wrong picks earn zero. The harder the call, the bigger the reward.

Point cap: 2.5 pts per pick (5 pts if double down used). This prevents anyone from gaming the system by stacking massive underdogs. Wild longshots still pay well — they just don't break the scoring scale. Your daily total from 5 picks is typically somewhere between 1 and 10 points.
Strategy layer

The confidence score

For each of your 5 picks, you assign a confidence score — 1 through 5. Each number is used exactly once. This number is your tiebreaker weapon, not a point multiplier. Choose wisely.

1
Least confident. Your shakiest pick.
2
Moderate confidence. A coin-flip you're leaning on.
3
High confidence. You like this one a lot.
4
Stone cold lock. You'd bet your lunch on it.
5
Absolute certainty. You'd bet your house on it.

Tiebreaker rule

What happens when you both pick the same side?

If you and your opponent pick the same player prop on the same side, the confidence scores decide who "owns" that pick — and the loser automatically gets flipped to the other side.

You
Jokic Assists — Under 9.5
Confidence: 4
VS
Opponent
Jokic Assists — Under 9.5
Confidence: 2

You win the tiebreaker (4 > 2). You keep the Under. Your opponent is automatically assigned the Over. Same line, opposite sides — now it's a zero-sum battle on that single prop.
